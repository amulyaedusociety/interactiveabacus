Japanese Abacus Simulator (Soroban)

soroban means counting tray. It is an abacus developed in Japan, based on the Chinese suanpan; imported to Japan in the 14th century

The soroban is composed of an odd number of columns or rods, each having beads: one separate bead having a value of five, called go-dama (五玉, ごだま, "five-bead") and four beads each having a value of one, called ichi-dama (一玉, いちだま, "one-bead"). Each set of beads of each rod is divided by a bar known as a reckoning bar.
